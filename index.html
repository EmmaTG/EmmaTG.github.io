A Little About Me

I started my studies in Mechanical Engineering at the University of the Witwatersrand in Johannesburg, South Africa. After completing this I began am master’s studies at the University of Cape Town (UCT). In 2020 I completed my PhD in Computation and Applied Mechanics at UCT. I then took up a postdoctoral position at Friedrich-Alexander-Universität (FAU) Erlangen-Nürnberg in 2021 where I work computational modelling and simulation of the brain.

I have used and explored many languages (Java, Python, C++), frameworks (Angular, React) and databases (MySQL, PostgreSQL, MongoDB). I also have experience in AWS. I enjoy creating software development solutions and am comfortable working in both the frontend and the backend development. I think cloud computing is a vital resource for any company or business and have enjoyed working with AWS products to provide cloud solutions. I believe that the skills I have learnt in academia can help create applications that provide real and meaningful change in businesses and individuals.

What Can I Offer

Doing post-graduate studies teaches you many skills that are transferrable to non-academic careers. Going through these degrees has taught me how to work as a team of collaborative researchers, presentation skills, communication skills (written and otherwise) as well as the perseverance to tackle big complex problems with an analytical mindset.

Life long learner

I started my Masters and PhD because of my longing to understand and learn more about a subject I found enjoyable. This desire to continually learn more and improve my skills is also present in my software developement learning process. I am continually looking for personal projects to do that can help me improve my current set of skills of gain some new one. I am currently working on improving my knowledge fo the React framework and hope to do some more AWS certifications in the future.

Logically minded

One reason I was drawn to the field of software development and computer sciences was due to the logical and methodical nature of the work. Tackling large research questions and problems during my time as a PhD student and now as a postdoc can only be done by approaching them in a logical and methodical way

Highly self motivated

You cannot complete a PhD without being motivated and being able to self-manage your time and yourself. Whilst doing a PhD you reach many stumbling block and road blocks, this is when you really learn how to be self-motivated.

Skilled in problem solving

I am not afraid of tackling big complex problems. All research projects begin with a very big question that seems impossible to answer. These are the times when knowing techniques of problem solving come in handy. DUrign my time in reasearch I have learnt how to break big problems down into smaller ones, how to carefully and methodically look for and solve problems and, most importantly, to know when to ask for help.
